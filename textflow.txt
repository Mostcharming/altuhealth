
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    APPOINTMENT CREATION & BOOKING FLOW
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[0] Enrollee Initiates Appointment Request (via ERP Portal/Mobile):
     - Enrollee logs into ERP system with Member ID credentials
     - Browse/search for providers:
        * By specialty (Cardiology, Orthopedics, etc.)
        * By location/facility
        * By insurance network availability
        * View provider credentials, ratings, experience
     - Select preferred provider/facility
     - System retrieves provider's available time slots
        ↓
[0a] Appointment Details Collection:
     - Service type selection:
        * Reason for visit/Chief complaint (required)
        * Estimated service category (Consultation, Diagnostic, Procedure, etc.)
        * Severity level (routine, urgent, non-emergency)
     - Appointment details captured:
        * Preferred appointment date & time
        * Appointment type (In-person, Telehealth, Hybrid)
        * Duration estimate (based on service type)
     - Enrollee information verified:
        * Name, DOB, Member ID
        * Current contact information (phone, email)
        * Dependent status (if appointment for dependent)
        * Insurance plan details (pulled from active enrollment)
     - Insurance coverage verification at booking time:
        * Verify plan is active/not suspended
        * Confirm no delinquent payments
        * Check policy effective dates
     - Emergency contact information (if applicable)
        ↓
[0b] Pre-Appointment Service Authorization Check (ERP):
     - Service type analysis:
        * Map chief complaint to medical service codes (CPT, ICD-10)
        * Determine service category (elective, routine, emergency, diagnostic)
     - Authorization requirement determination:
        * Check plan requirements for service type
        * Query authorization lookup table:
           - Does this service require prior authorization? (Yes/No)
           - Is provider network status relevant? (In-network vs Out-of-network)
           - Are there service-specific preconditions?
     - Eligibility cross-check:
        * Verify enrollee age appropriateness for service (pediatric, adult-only)
        * Check for coverage exclusions specific to service
        * Verify plan covers this service (not excluded)
        ↓
[0c] Authorization Decision at Booking Stage:
    ├─ Service DOES NOT require prior auth (routine/preventive)
    │  └─ Proceed to [0d] - Confirm Appointment
    │
    └─ Service REQUIRES prior authorization (elective/complex)
       └─ Proceed to [0c-1] - Collect Pre-Auth Data
        ↓
[0c-1] Collect Pre-Authorization Data at Booking:
     - For authorization-required services, collect additional info:
        * Relevant medical history:
           - Previous treatments/surgeries for this condition
           - Current medications related to condition
           - Relevant lab results or imaging (if available)
           - Chronic conditions that affect treatment
        * Specific clinical information:
           - Duration/frequency of treatment (if multi-visit)
           - Specific procedures planned (if known)
           - Treatment goals/objectives
        * Supporting documentation upload option:
           - Recent test results
           - Imaging reports
           - Medical records from other providers
        * Authorization request status set to "Pre-Pending"
        ↓
[0d] Appointment Confirmation & Booking:
     - Appointment slot reserved:
        * Appointment ID generated
        * Appointment status: "Confirmed" or "Pending Authorization"
        * Timestamp recorded (booking date/time)
     - For NON-authorization services: Status = "Confirmed"
     - For AUTHORIZATION services: Status = "Pending Authorization"
     - Confirmation details:
        * Appointment confirmation number sent to enrollee
        * SMS/Email confirmation sent with:
           - Appointment date/time
           - Provider details
           - Facility location & directions
           - Pre-visit instructions
           - Cancellation policy
        * Appointment stored in Enrollee's ERP schedule
        * Provider calendar updated (pending if auth required)
        ↓
[0e] For Authorization-Required Services - Submit to HMO:
     - If pre-auth required, forward collected data:
        * Appointment ID linked to authorization request
        * All collected service/clinical information
        * Enrollee eligibility snapshot (active plan, coverage, limits)
        * Provider information
     - Request status: "Submitted for Pre-Authorization"
     - Proceed to [3] HMO ERP System Validation
        ↓
        
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    ENROLLMENT → PRE-AUTHORIZATION FLOW
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[3] HMO ERP System Validation (Pre-Authorization Received):
     - Data validation:
        * Verify request format and completeness
        * Validate medical codes (ICD-10, CPT, HCPCS) if provided
        * Check provider/facility network status
     - Enrollee eligibility verification:
        * Confirm enrollment status (active/inactive)
        * Verify member is not in grace period or suspended
        * Check for policy termination or cancellation
     - Plan coverage & benefits verification:
        * Confirm service covered under plan
        * Check if service requires prior authorization
        * Verify service not excluded from plan
     - Benefit limits & utilization check:
        * Calculate remaining annual deductible
        * Check visit limits (e.g., PT visits per year)
        * Verify remaining out-of-pocket maximum
        * Review service-specific limits
        * Cross-reference with dependent limits (if applicable)
     - Concurrent review checks:
        * Check for existing authorizations for same service
        * Verify no conflicting treatments authorized
        * Review patient's recent claim history
     - Validation result:
        * All checks passed → Proceed to [4]
        * Validation errors found → Return error to provider with details
        * Suspicious patterns detected → Flag for manual review
        ↓
[4] Decision Point:
    ├── Auto-Approve (if rules match) 
    └── Manual Review (if complex case)
              ↓
        HMO Admin reviews
              ↓
        Approve / Reject / Query / Suspend
        ↓
[4a] Handle Query/Additional Info Request:
     - Provider/Enrollee submits additional documents
     - Medical review team evaluates
     - Final decision made
        ↓
[5] Response sent to Provider & Enrollee:
     - Auth Code generated (if Approved)
        * Authorization valid from date to date
        * Authorization covers specific services/procedures
        * Coverage percentage noted (80%, 90%, etc.)
     - Rejection reason documented (if Rejected)
     - Appeal instructions provided (if Denied)
     - Appointment status updated in ERP:
        * "Confirmed & Authorized" (if approved)
        * "Appointment Cancelled - Not Authorized" (if rejected)
     - Notification sent to Enrollee:
        * Authorization status
        * Expected out-of-pocket costs
        * Any pre-visit requirements
        ↓
        
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                      VISIT EXECUTION & SERVICE DELIVERY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[1] Patient (Enrollee) Visits Provider (At Appointment):
     - Enrollee checks in at provider facility
     - Check-in process:
        * Verify identity (ID/Member card)
        * Confirm appointment details in provider system
        * Update contact information if changed
     - Pre-visit data collection:
        * Chief complaint/reason for visit
        * Vital signs recorded (temperature, BP, HR, weight, etc.)
        * Current medications review
        * Allergy review/confirmation
        * Initial assessment completed
     - Insurance verification at visit:
        * Confirm coverage is still active
        * Verify no changes to deductible/copay status
        * Check eligibility in real-time
     - Provider determines if service aligns with authorization (if pre-auth obtained):
        * Compare actual services planned vs. authorized services
        * Update any additional services if medically necessary
     - Financial responsibility collection (if applicable):
        * Copay collected based on plan/service type
        * Deductible portions collected if applicable
        * Co-insurance percentage explained to enrollee
        * Payment methods accepted
        ↓
[1a] In-Visit Service Determination & Real-Time Authorization Check:
     - Provider assesses patient during visit:
        * Performs initial examination
        * Determines actual services/procedures needed
        * Identifies any additional/modified procedures
     - Service type classification (real-time):
        * Original planned service matches assessment? (Yes/No)
        * Additional services identified? (If yes, determine authorization need)
        * Service complexity level confirmed
        * Duration/frequency finalized
     - For MODIFIED/ADDITIONAL services (not in original pre-auth):
        * Determine if new services require authorization
        * If authorization required:
           - Flag for real-time/stat authorization request
           - Check if can proceed under emergency exception
           - Escalate to HMO if not authorized
        * If authorization not required:
           - Proceed with service delivery
     - Authorization status verification:
        * Provider queries: Is this service covered under the appointment authorization?
        * Verify authorization code still valid
        * Check authorization hasn't been exhausted/used
        ↓
[1b] Service Authorization Status Decision:
    ├─ Service covered under existing authorization
    │  └─ Proceed to [6] Service Delivery
    │
    ├─ Service NOT covered under existing authorization
    │  └─ Request real-time authorization to HMO
    │     (provider system submits stat request)
    │     - HMO reviews in real-time (15-30 min turnaround)
    │     - Approval → Proceed to [6]
    │     - Denial → Obtain written consent from enrollee to proceed
    │             (patient assumes financial responsibility)
    │
    └─ Emergency service (life-threatening)
       └─ Proceed immediately to [6]
          (Concurrent review occurs post-treatment)
        ↓
[6] Provider Delivers Service:
     - All authorized services provided:
        * Procedures/treatments documented with timestamps
        * All relevant clinical codes recorded (ICD-10, CPT, HCPCS)
        * Provider notes documented in real-time
        * Medication administered/prescribed (if applicable)
     - Post-service data collection:
        * Final assessment/outcomes documented
        * Patient education provided
        * Follow-up instructions given
        * Discharge summary created (if applicable)
        ↓
[6a] Medical History Updated:
     - Diagnoses recorded:
        * ICD-10 codes for conditions identified/treated
        * Primary diagnosis
        * Secondary diagnoses
     - Procedures documented:
        * CPT codes for procedures performed
        * Procedure notes/details
        * Start/end times
     - Medications prescribed:
        * Medication names, dosages, frequencies
        * Duration of prescriptions
        * Special instructions
     - Visit notes stored:
        * Provider's clinical assessment
        * Treatment plan
        * Future care recommendations
     - Health records updated in Enrollee profile:
        * All data synced to ERP medical history
        * Records available for future provider access
        * Patient can view visit summary in ERP portal
        ↓
[6b] Benefit Utilization Updated (Real-Time):
     - Deductible amount applied:
        * Calculate deductible portion of services
        * Update remaining deductible balance
     - Co-pay/Co-insurance recorded:
        * Amount collected at visit
        * Amount owed by enrollee
     - Benefit limits decremented:
        * Annual visit limits (specialist visits, PT sessions, etc.)
        * Out-of-pocket maximums tracked
     - Annual usage tracked per service type:
        * Services logged against annual limits
        * Concurrent utilization checked
     - Authorization utilization tracked:
        * If pre-auth was issued with visit limits, decrement usage
        * Authorization status updated
        ↓
[6c] Checkout & Payment at Provider:
     - Final visit charges confirmed:
        * Amount billed to insurance
        * Patient responsibility amount due
     - Payment collected:
        * Remaining copay/coinsurance collected
        * Out-of-pocket amounts settled
     - Visit completion recorded:
        * Appointment status changed to "Completed"
        * Actual visit date/time recorded
        * Provider attestation of visit
     - Receipts/Discharge documents provided to enrollee
        ↓

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                    CLAIM SUBMISSION & PROCESSING FLOW
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[7] Claim Submission (Post-Treatment):
[7] Claim Submission (Post-Treatment):
     - Provider prepares claim with actual visit data:
        * Claim ID generated
        * Appointment ID linked to claim
        * Authorization code referenced (if pre-auth obtained)
        * Visit date verified against appointment date
     - Claim line items created:
        * Each service/procedure as separate line item
        * Service codes (CPT, ICD-10, HCPCS) from actual delivery
        * Quantities and units of service
        * Charges for each service
     - Provider information submitted:
        * Provider credentials/NPI
        * Facility details
        * Tax ID
     - Enrollee information submitted:
        * Member ID
        * DOB
        * Plan details
     - Claim transmission:
        * Electronic claim sent to HMO
        * Claim status: "Received"
        * Timestamp recorded
        ↓
[8] Claim Adjudication & Verification:
     - Claim validation against authorization:
        * If pre-auth obtained: Verify claim services match authorization
        * Check authorization code validity
        * Verify dates of service within authorization period
     - Service code verification:
        * CPT codes valid and appropriate
        * ICD-10 codes valid and match services
        * Service bundling rules applied
     - Amount verification against approved limits:
        * Billed amounts vs. allowed amounts
        * Apply plan fee schedules
        * Apply any negotiated rates with provider
        * Calculate enrollee responsibility
     - Duplicate claim check:
        * Compare with historical claims (same enrollee, provider, dates)
        * Identify potential duplicate submissions
        ↓
[8a] Admin Claim Vetting & Fraud Detection:
     - Verify enrollee actually attended visit:
        * Cross-reference appointment record (check status: "Completed")
        * Verify check-in record at provider facility
        * Match claim service date with appointment date
     - Cross-reference services billed vs. authorized services:
        * Compare CPT codes on claim vs. authorization
        * Identify any unbilled authorized services
        * Identify any non-authorized services billed
        * Flag variances for review
     - Compare claimed procedures with medical history records:
        * Verify procedures align with documented diagnoses
        * Check for illogical service combinations
        * Review against enrollee's medical pattern
     - Verify provider credentials and authorization status:
        * Provider NPI active and not excluded
        * Provider still in network (as of service date)
        * Facility credentials current
        * License/certifications valid
     - Check for service bundling abuse:
        * Identify unbundled procedures (should be bundled)
        * Check for component billing (billing parts separately)
        * Apply bundling rules per plan
     - Validate dates of service:
        * No future dates on claim
        * Sequential visits logical (not billing same service twice same day)
        * Service dates within claim submission window
     - Review for billing anomalies or patterns:
        * Unusual high volumes of procedures
        * Services rarely seen on this provider's claims
        * Charges significantly higher than market rates
        ↓
[8b] Verification Decision:
    ├── Verified & Approved (all checks passed)
    │  └─ Proceed to [9] - Approved for Payment
    │
    ├── Flag for Review (inconsistencies found)
    │  └─ Proceed to [8c] - Vetting Process
    │
    └── Reject (fraud/billing errors detected)
       └─ Proceed to [9] - Denied with documentation
        ↓
[8c] Vetting Process (for flagged claims):
     - Senior claims analyst reviews flagged claims:
        * Identify specific discrepancies
        * Determine severity (minor billing issue vs. potential fraud)
     - Contact provider for clarification if needed:
        * Request supporting documentation
        * Ask provider to explain service rationale
        * Request itemized bills if needed
     - Request additional documentation from enrollee:
        * Confirm they received services
        * Request provider receipts
        * Obtain medical records if provider not responsive
     - Medical necessity review:
        * Consult clinical guidelines for diagnoses
        * Verify procedures align with clinical standards
        * Review against similar member claims
     - Final determination made:
        * Claim approved (issue resolved)
        * Claim approved with adjustment (correct charges)
        * Claim denied (fraud indicators)
        * Claim queried (awaiting response)
        ↓

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                      PAYMENT & SETTLEMENT PROCESSING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[9] Claims Processing Decision:
[9] Claims Processing Decision:
    ├── Approved for Payment (verified & within limits)
    │  - Calculate benefit percentage (80%, 90%, etc.)
    │  - Calculate insurance payment amount
    │  - Calculate enrollee balance
    │  - Proceed to [9a] - Payment Batch Creation
    │
    ├── Partial Payment (coverage limits or partial service verification)
    │  - Some services covered, some not
    │  - Calculate partial payment amount
    │  - Document which services excluded and why
    │  - Proceed to [9a] - Payment Batch Creation (reduced amount)
    │
    ├── Denied (reason documented - fraud, unauthorized service, etc)
    │  - Document denial reason (fraud, not covered, out-of-network, etc)
    │  - No insurance payment
    │  - Enrollee responsible for full amount
    │  - Proceed to [9b] - Denial Notification
    │
    └── Queried (requesting more information from provider)
       - Hold claim in pending status
       - Set response deadline (15-30 days)
       - Notify provider of needed information
        ↓
[9a] Payment Batch Creation:
     - All approved/partial payment claims collected:
        * Filter for claims marked "Approved for Payment"
        * Filter for claims marked "Partial Payment"
        * Exclude denied/queried claims
     - Batches organized by:
        * Provider (group claims by provider for efficiency)
        * Payment method (ACH, Check, Wire, etc.)
        * Claim type (Insurance payment, Coordination of benefits, etc.)
     - Batch summary generated with total amounts:
        * Total insurance payment amount
        * Total enrollee responsibility
        * Number of claims in batch
        * Number of affected enrollees
     - Quality control check on batch integrity:
        * Verify all claims in batch have valid approval
        * Verify amounts calculated correctly
        * Verify no duplicate claims included
        * Verify provider information is valid
     - Batch marked ready for processing:
        * Batch ID generated
        * Batch status: "Ready for Approval"
        * Batch created timestamp recorded
        ↓
[9b] Denial Notification:
     - Enrollee notified of claim denial:
        * Denial reason clearly explained
        * Service/procedure affected
        * Specific plan limitation or exclusion referenced
        * Appeal rights communicated
     - Provider notified of denial:
        * Allows provider to follow up with enrollee
        * May resubmit if documentation was issue
     - Appeal instructions provided:
        * Appeal period timeframe
        * Appeal contact information
        * Documentation to submit with appeal
        ↓
[10] Payment Processing:
     - Insurance company pays provider:
        * Via selected payment method
        * Using pre-authorized payment information
        * According to plan's payment terms
     - Enrollee notified of visit charges/balance:
        * Explanation of Benefits (EOB) generated
        * Breakdown of charges, insurance payment, enrollee responsibility
        * Amount due from enrollee (if any)
        * Payment instructions if enrollee portion due
     - Payment reconciliation initiated:
        * Begin preparation for settlement
        ↓
[10a] Payment Batch Processing Execution:
     - Batch authorized for payment by finance manager:
        * Finance/Billing manager reviews batch
        * Verifies batch integrity and amounts
        * Approves batch for payment execution
        * Records authorization (who, when, signature)
     - Payment method initiated:
        * Bank transfer (ACH/Wire):
           - Initiate electronic transfer to provider bank
           - Use provider's pre-registered banking information
           - Generate transfer reference number
        * Check payment:
           - Print check for provider
           - Require dual signatures
           - Add check reference to batch
        * Digital wallet/Direct deposit:
           - Route payment to provider's preferred digital account
           - Generate digital transfer confirmation
     - Payment reference numbers generated for tracking:
        * Unique reference number per payment
        * Links payment to original batch
        * Links payment to provider
     - Transaction logs created:
        * Record payment initiation
        * Record payment method
        * Record timestamp
        * Record authorization person
     - Payment status updated in system:
        * Batch status changed: "Ready for Approval" → "Processing"
        * Individual claim status: "Approved" → "In Payment"
        ↓
[10b] Payment Settlement & Reconciliation:
     - Batch payment status monitored:
        * Track payment through banking system
        * Monitor for failed transactions
     - Failed transactions identified and handled:
        * Retry logic applied (ACH retries, etc.)
        * Provider contacted if banking information invalid
        * Alternate payment method arranged
     - Payment confirmation received from bank/payment processor:
        * Bank confirms payment sent/processed
        * Delivery confirmation obtained
        * Receipts collected
     - Reconciliation with GL (General Ledger):
        * Payment amount matched to GL entry
        * Payment date recorded
        * Payment account codes recorded
        * Batch reconciliation to accounting system
     - Payment status updated (processing → completed/settled):
        * Batch status: "Processing" → "Settled"
        * Individual claims: "In Payment" → "Paid"
        * Final payment timestamp recorded
     - Provider notified of payment receipt:
        * Notification email sent with payment details
        * Payment remittance advice attached
        * Claim-to-payment mapping provided
        ↓
[10c] Batch Reporting & Audit Trail:
     - Payment batch report generated:
        * Summary of all payments in batch
        * Total amounts paid
        * Provider breakdown
        * Claim count
     - Transaction receipts stored:
        * Payment confirmation documents
        * Bank receipts
        * Check images/copies
     - Audit trail documented:
        * Who created batch (claims processor)
        * Who authorized batch (finance manager)
        * When approved (timestamp)
        * Amount approved
        * Who processed payment (payment processor)
        * When processed (timestamp)
     - Batch reconciliation report finalized:
        * Batch reconciliation to original claims
        * Reconciliation to payment executed
        * Final reconciliation status confirmed
     - Records archived for compliance:
        * All batch documentation stored
        * Records retained per regulatory requirements
        * Records made available for audits
        ↓

                          APPEAL PROCESS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[11] Appeal Process (if Pre-Auth Denied or Claim Denied):
     - Enrollee/Provider can appeal within timeframe:
        * Appeal period typically 30-90 days from denial
        * Appeals submitted via ERP portal or provider portal
        * Appeal request must include member ID, claim/auth details
     - Appeal documentation prepared:
        * Enrollee/provider includes additional clinical evidence
        * Justification for why service should be covered
        * Medical literature supporting necessity
     - Case reviewed by senior medical reviewer:
        * Medical reviewer independent from original decision maker
        * Clinical guidelines reviewed
        * Enrollee's specific circumstances considered
        * Expert opinion obtained if complex case
     - Appeal decision communicated:
        * Approval: Original decision reversed, service/claim approved
        * Affirmed: Original denial upheld
        * Partial approval: Service/claim approved partially
        * Timeframe: Decision within 30 days (expedited if urgent)
     - Next steps if appeal denied:
        * External review option available (if applicable by law)
        * Information provided on how to file external appeal
        ↓
